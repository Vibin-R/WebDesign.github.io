Mark Zuckerberg

Here's a time-line of Mark Zuckerberg's life:


  • 1984 - Mark Elliot Zuckerberg was born on May 14, 1984, in White Plains, New York. He grew up in Dobbs Ferry, New York, with his parents, Edward and Karen Zuckerberg, and three sisters.

  • 2004 - While studying at Harvard University, Zuckerberg launched a social networking website called "Thefacebook" on February 4, 2004. It initially started as a platform exclusively for Harvard students but soon expanded to other universities and colleges.

  • 2005 - Thefacebook gained significant popularity and underwent a name change to simply "Facebook." Zuckerberg dropped out of Harvard in his sophomore year to focus on developing and expanding the social media platform.

  • 2006 - Facebook opened its membership to anyone over the age of 13 with a valid email address. The platform continued to grow rapidly and attracted the attention of investors and businesses.

  • 2007 - Facebook launched the Facebook Platform, allowing third-party developers to build applications and games within the site. This move significantly contributed to the platform's engagement and user base.

  • 2010 - The film "The Social Network" was released, which depicted the founding of Facebook and the early years of Zuckerberg's journey. Although the film received critical acclaim, Zuckerberg expressed that it contained fictional elements.

  • 2012 - Facebook went public with one of the largest initial public offerings (IPO) in history. The company was listed on the NASDAQ stock exchange, and Zuckerberg became one of the youngest self-made billionaires in the world.

  • 2014 - In a notable acquisition, Facebook acquired the messaging service WhatsApp for approximately $19 billion, marking one of the most significant purchases in the tech industry.

  • 2016 - Zuckerberg and his wife, Priscilla Chan, launched the Chan Zuckerberg Initiative, a philanthropic organization aimed at advancing human potential and promoting equality in areas such as education, science, and criminal justice reform.

  • 2018 - Facebook faced significant controversies related to user privacy and the Cambridge Analytica data scandal. These events led to increased scrutiny of the platform's handling of user data and privacy concerns.

  • 2021 - Zuckerberg announced that Facebook would be rebranding as Meta, reflecting the company's broader focus on building a metaverse a virtual shared space where people can interact in various ways.
